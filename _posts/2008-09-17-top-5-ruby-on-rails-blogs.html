--- layout: post title: Top 5 Ruby on Rails Blogs tags: [] status: draft type: post published: false meta: _edit_last: '1' --- When interviewing for a new job, more often I am getting asked which blogs do I read on a regular basis. Its one of my favorite questions and I think one of the most telling to a potential client or employer. Instead of doing some hem hawing the next time it is asked, I thought I should pick my top ruby blogs in advanced. Here are my picks in no particular order. Ruby Inside - Ruby Inside has been around around the Ruby scene as long as I can remember. Written by Peter Cooper Ruby Inside will keep you up to date on the general goings ons in the Ruby community. Posts are almost usually links to new Ruby apps, services, or blog posts, as opposed to tutorials or articles, but if you want to know what's going on this is the blog to read. Jay Fields' Thoughts - Jay Fields blog is the masters course of Ruby blogs. Jay will take you in depth in both code and business strategy and does a great job at both. His posts are thought out and articulate. He has a lot of experience in traditional software development and this comes across nicely in his writing. Jay is responsible for a lot my business practices I currently maintain and although I don't think he bills himself as an XP or Agile programmer, Jay is innovative and logical when it comes to programming styles and etiquette. Ryan's Scrapes - Ryan Daigle rails blog is a must for anyone doing Rails development. Ryan's blog focuses on highlighting what is coming down the pipe in new versions of Ruby on Rails is series of posts called "What's new is Edge Rails." In his series Ryan will go through the new major enhancements that have recently made their way in to the trunk or Rails. Even for those not running Edge Rails, "What's New..." gives readers a nice condensed format for following Rails development up to a release.
